Q: I'm struggling with procrastination and can't seem to start my assignments until the last minute. Any tips?
A: Try the "2-minute rule" - if something takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. For larger tasks, break them into smaller 15-minute chunks. Also, create a dedicated study space away from distractions. - Sarah J., Academic Counselor
Q: I feel overwhelmed by social situations at university. How can I make friends without feeling anxious?
A: Start small - join study groups or clubs related to your interests. This gives you a natural conversation starter. Remember, most people are understanding and many feel the same way you do. Practice self-compassion and take it one interaction at a time. - Dr. Emily R.
Q: How do I balance work, studies, and personal life without burning out?
A: Time-blocking is your friend! Schedule specific times for work, study, and relaxation. Don't forget to include buffer time for unexpected tasks. Most importantly, guard your sleep and don't skip meals - your brain needs fuel to function. - Michael C., Peer Mentor
